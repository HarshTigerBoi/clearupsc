# ClearUPSC Notes Style Guide

## Goal

Every NCERT-backed topic should feel like a brilliant teacher is sitting beside a 13-year-old and explaining the chapter patiently.

The goal is not to copy NCERT. The goal is to convert every important idea into original, easy language, then compress it into exam-ready notes.

## Required Structure

Each high-quality topic note must have these parts:

1. **Easy Chapter Explanation**
   - Explain the chapter's concepts in simple language.
   - Cover every major idea from the NCERT chapter.
   - Use short paragraphs and clear transitions.
   - Assume the student is intelligent but new to the topic.

2. **Analogy Map**
   - Give analogies for the core ideas.
   - The analogy must map correctly to the actual concept.
   - Avoid vague motivational analogies.

3. **Concise UPSC Notes**
   - Turn the easy explanation into Prelims and Mains notes.
   - Include definitions, roles, features, issues, examples, and balanced conclusions.

4. **Short Notes**
   - One-page last-day revision version.
   - Use tight bullet points.
   - Keep only the highest-yield facts and frameworks.

5. **Answer Template**
   - Give one 150-word or 250-word model skeleton.
   - Must include intro, body and conclusion logic.

6. **Questions The Student Can Now Answer**
   - List likely UPSC-style conceptual questions.

## Non-Negotiable Quality Rules

- Do not paste full NCERT text.
- Do not produce generic notes that could fit any topic.
- Do not skip subtopics because they are difficult.
- Do not fake facts, answer keys, judgments or official sources.
- Use words a teenager can understand, then add UPSC vocabulary after the meaning is clear.
- Every analogy must teach the exact concept, not just decorate the page.
- Every note must have both understanding and revision value.

## Current Format Pilot

The first upgraded topic is:

- `gs2_polity_judiciary`
- `gs2_polity_supreme_court`

This pilot uses the format:

- Easy chapter explanation
- Analogy map
- Concise UPSC notes
- Short notes
- Mains answer template
- Practice questions
